Spiritual Formation

Spiritual Formation is central to the education of students at Bishop Allison Theological College. Students participate in services of morning and evening prayer every weekday, and the college also holds a Holy Communion service every Wednesday afternoon. Students assist in leading these services in order to allow them to implement practically the concepts and practices that they are learning in their classes. These regular worship services help keep our academic pursuits at the college situated in the context of the worship of our Triune God. On Sundays, students participate in worship services in local churches around the college.

Also, all students participate in mentorship groups led by faculty mentors. Students meet each week in small groups with a member of the teaching staff in order to discuss their spiritual, academic, and personal development. Students are encouraged to discuss any questions they may have about their studies, their spiritual growth, or any other issues during these regular sessions.

Additionally, the college's faculty educate and lead the students in various spiritual disciplines, such as fasting, Bible study, and personal devotions. Our goal is for the students to become accustomed to the practice of spiritual disciplines during their time at the college so that these disciplines will become habits they will practice throughout their lives.
